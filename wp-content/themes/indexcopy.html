Why should you take a workshop?

You've just spent a small fortune on your first DSLR and lens. If you don't know what it can do and how it can do it, it's going to end up a very expensive point and shoot camera! Learning how to use it properly will not only enhance your photography it will make it very pleasureable, instead of a frustrating experience.

Maybe make money on the side or turn pro

The more proficient you are the more confident you will become. Once your confidence has grown you will feel more able to offer your expertise to paying customers!

Just to be the best you can be

Not everybody wants to be a full time pro. Some people just want to achieve excellence in the craft. Taking on a workshop is probably one of the best steps you can take as it gives you hands on, real life, experience.

Who Should Take a workshop?

Total Beginners

If this is your first paddle in the photography pond these workshops will be ideal for you. The fastest, and arguably one of the best, ways to get up and running is to get hands on experience, doing it yourself.

Workshops are designed for all levels and can be tailored to suit your needs. Covering all aspects of camera techniques and settings. Plain English explanations to everything so it’s easy to understand and not get bogged down with technical jargon.

Our promise is that by the end of the workshop you will have created at least one image you will be totally proud of!

Intermediate

Aimed at those that have done some photography and have a basic understanding but maybe you have been stuck in auto mode, too frightened to change that dial.

Now’s the time to try “full-manual!!!” Get creative!!

Groups of Friends

It may well be that you are quite happy with using your camera and just want a fun day out with a pro. There’s nothing quite like having an expert on hand to answer questions and give a helping hand when needed.

Corporate Team Building Days

Getting your team to let their hair down and enjoy a fun day of photography is great for team building. Challenges will be set where everybody will need to work together to create their masterpieces.

People Looking for a New Career

If it is paid work you are looking for then you will certainly need to get up to speed with all that is photography. It is not an easy career path, you will be competing with thousands of others, but, done correctly, it is one of the most rewarding careers anybody could have.

People Wanting To Improve Their Editing Skills

Editing your images is one of the most essential skills you need to have. Our Lightroom workshops go through the basics of editing a raw image and move on to a few advanced techniques. By the end of the workshop you will know, among other things, what the tools do and how to use them.
